Empowering UC San Diego to become AI enabled

Mission & Vision

UC San Diego is building an AI-enabled campus where 73,000+ students, faculty, and staff can confidently use trusted, secure, and responsible AI to improve teaching, research, and operations — while protecting ethics, equity, and university values. What began as TritonGPT has grown into TritonAI: a shared ecosystem of platform infrastructure, specialized assistants, developer APIs, instructional tools, and the early scaffolding for governed agent workflows.

AI Strategy, Priorities & Focus Areas

UC San Diego's AI strategy is built around a few practical principles:

Governed infrastructure for sensitive work

TritonAI separates local/self-hosted capacity from approved cloud routes so projects can match model choice to data sensitivity, cost, and task complexity. The San Diego Supercomputer Center hosts on-prem models; LiteLLM Enterprise governs routing, metering, and access.

Shared access instead of isolated pilots

Zero-variable-cost access makes experimentation easier for more people. The goal is not a small number of premium tools, but broad and equitable access to useful ones.

Solve specific pain points

The strongest use cases have been concrete: contract review (91% faster), job description drafting (83% faster), campus support, scheduling, and phishing analysis. Focused tools create trust faster than generic chat.

Meet people in the workflow

TritonAI appears in Blink, public websites, the campus mobile app, teaching tools, developer projects, and departmental applications. Adoption improves when AI shows up where work is already happening.

Stay model-agnostic

The architecture uses gateways, canonical model routes, connectors, usage reporting, and governance rather than a single provider. 20+ models are available through the governed gateway.

Prepare for agency

The TritonAI Harness is the execution layer that turns model inference into governed, tool-using AI workers. APIs, reusable skills, MCP connectors, model observability, and access monitoring make the shift from answering questions to completing supervised multi-step work.

Governance & Responsible Use

UC San Diego follows UC systemwide Responsible AI principles and applies them to local campus practices. Oversight comes from the UC AI Council, the Council of Data Stewards (CDS), and local governance through the AI Development Workgroup. The TritonAI platform enforces governance through LiteLLM gateway controls: usage monitoring, access scopes, model routing policies, and recharge mechanics. A UC system-wide Claude Enterprise agreement is in progress through UCOP and Anthropic.
