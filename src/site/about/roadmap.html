TritonAI Roadmap

How We Got Here

TritonGPT began as an internal innovation effort led by UC San Diego IT Services and funded through the Office of the Chancellor. The initiative grew out of a simple idea: build a secure, campus-controlled AI tool that protects university data, operates inside our own infrastructure, and directly supports the day-to-day work of staff.

To meet those expectations, UC San Diego partnered with the San Diego Supercomputer Center to host TritonGPT on university-owned systems. This approach ensures:

  • Data stays on campus
  • Access is secured through campus Single Sign-On
  • No data is shared with commercial AI providers
  • Model updates and improvements are fully governed by UC San Diego

What started as a pilot has quickly grown into one of UC San Diego’s most widely adopted digital tools, supported by training, documentation, and an expanding catalog of AI Assistants designed for campus-specific tasks.

Where We Are Going

TritonGPT continues to evolve as a core productivity tool for the university. Future work focuses on deeper capabilities, stronger integrations, and broader AI literacy across campus.

Priority Areas

  • New task-focused AI Assistants for HR, communications, financial workflows, and operational support
  • Secure integration with additional structured data sources
  • Expanded editing and productivity tools for drafting, summaries, rewrites, and content organization
  • More training options, including workshops, role-based learning, and ongoing support
  • Continuous model and infrastructure improvements at SDSC

The goal is to make TritonGPT a reliable, secure, and intuitive tool that supports the work of staff across UC San Diego.

TritonGPT Initiative at a Glance

2025: Expansion and Acceleration

Rapid Development of New AI Assistants (June to December)
The TritonGPT team is focused on scale, refinement, and responsiveness to campus needs. Work includes:
  • New task-specific AI Assistants for administrative and operational workflows
  • Faster performance through SDSC infrastructure upgrades
  • Enhanced search and knowledge retrieval
  • Improvements to the interface and user experience
  • Continuous updates based on campus feedback
  • Alignment with UC responsible AI guidelines

2024: Campuswide Launch and Training Expansion

Campuswide Availability (Spring)
All campus employees gain access to:
  • UC San Diego Assistant
  • Job Description Helper
  • Fund Manager Coach (new release)
Office Hours for Support and Feedback (April to May)
Weekly Zoom sessions give users direct access to the TritonGPT team for help, questions, and feedback.

AI Essentials II Training (April)
New training launches in the UC Learning Center, including:
  • Asynchronous modules
  • Instructor-led sessions
  • Deep dives on TritonGPT features and capabilities
Phased Rollout (March)
The first campus-wide release introduces:
  • UC San Diego Assistant
  • General AI Assistant
  • Job Description Helper

2023: Foundation and Early Adoption

AI Essentials I Training (November)
An introductory, asynchronous course on AI concepts and terminology launches in the UC Learning Center.

Pilot Launch (October)
More than 400 staff participate in beta testing and provide early feedback that shapes the platform.

Development Begins (June)
UC San Diego IT Services, supported by the Office of the Chancellor, begins building TritonGPT on SDSC’s secure, campus-owned infrastructure.
