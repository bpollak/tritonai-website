Prompt Library

Large language models, like those used by TritonGPT, require instructions in order to fulfill a request from the user. These instructions, known as “prompts,” can encompass a wide array of potential use cases. The following library showcases prompts that you can explore and try within TritonGPT.

We encourage you to explore different prompting strategies, not only to find one that might match your use case, but also to inspire you to develop your own prompts.

How to Use the Prompt Library

Browse the categories below. Expand a prompt to see exactly:

  • When to use it
  • Which assistant works best
  • A ready-to-copy example
  • Links to the Everyday I AI video series

Informational Prompts

These prompts help you analyze, explain, structure, or extract information in a clear, accurate format. Use them when you want TritonGPT to retrieve, classify, or transform information, not invent new content.

Comparison & Contrast

Purpose

Use this when you need to qquickly analyze two or more concepts, entities, or data points and clearly highlight their key similarities and differences.

Best TGPT Assistant

General Assistant

Example Prompt

Compare and contrast the economic policies of [Concept A] and [Concept B] in a two-column table. Include similarities, key differences, and real-world implications.
