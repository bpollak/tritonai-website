# Public content governance

The website is the public source of truth. Presentation material may inform it, but a slide is not automatically approved website content.

## Publishable content

Public content can include approved capabilities, status-based roadmap items, service-owner responsibilities, public architecture, learning paths, and use-case descriptions with human oversight and measurement plans.

## Content kept out of the public build

- individual employment arrangements or staffing allocations
- unapproved savings, accuracy, adoption, or return-on-investment claims
- speculative workforce, finance, or other sensitive proof-of-concept details
- exact dates that have not become public commitments
- sensitive incident-response or security-control details
- vendor or coding-tool preference comparisons

## Status definitions

- `Shipped`: publicly available and supported at the stated scope
- `Pilot`: bounded testing with named ownership and human oversight
- `In development`: active work without a committed public launch date
- `Exploring`: discovery only; no delivery commitment

## Review contract

High-change content includes an owner, source, audience, canonical URL, related slide reference, and `lastReviewed` date. Use cases additionally include status, measurement period, data classification, human oversight, and measurable outcome. The build blocks missing fields, and validation reports stale content.

For video, use controls without autoplay, provide captions and a transcript, include a descriptive poster or text alternative, and preserve the same information for people who prefer reduced motion.
